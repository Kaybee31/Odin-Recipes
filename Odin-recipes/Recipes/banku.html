Banku

Banku is a local term in the Ga-Dangme (Ga)-language coined for a cooked slightly fermented mixture of corn-dough and cassava-dough made into dumplings to serve.

It is distinctively a Ga-Dangme term. There are similar tonal terms with different meaning in the Ga-language, such as Inku (for pomade in the Ga-language), Ashanku (for a variant of a plantain fritter called 'Tatale' in the Ga-language), and many others ending in 'Ku'.

Banku is different from any of the Akple product forms indigenous to the Ewes. It is a Ghanaian of GaDangme (or Ga) descent dish which is cooked by a mixture of fermented corn and cassava dough in hot water into a smooth, whitish paste,served with soup, okra stew or a pepper sauce with fish.

Ingredients

  • Cassava Dough
  • Corn Dough
  • water
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Peel and chop the cassava,then combined with maize grains and soaked for a day
  2. After draining the water,the cassava and maize are milled into a smooth fine dough
  3. In a deep stainless steel pan,combine the corn dough and cassava dough and add some water.
  4. Stir constantly over a medium flame until the slurry begins to boil,continue to mix slowly until the slurry resembles a dough
  5. Knead your dough with your wooden spatula until it is smooth
  6. Add enough water to almost cover the surface of the dough in the pan
  7. Allow to boil to ensure equal cooking
  8. Re-centering,wetting,and kneading the dough until it is soft and evenly cooked
  9. Carry on this manner until the Banku from the deep stainless pan is completed
  10. You can serve the Banku with Okro soup and fresh fish.It can also be served with soup,stew,or pepper with any meat or fish of your choice./li>
